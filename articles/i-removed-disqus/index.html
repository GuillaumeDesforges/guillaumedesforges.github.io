Why I removed Disqus from my blog

25-10-2020 - Tags: meta
> Honestly, not much to say

I went back to my personal web pages to add an update on one of my article "Are Data Scientists actually developpers ?".

At the same time, I decided to remove Disqus from my website.

What is Disqus

Disqus is a plateform that allows you to add the ability for your visitors to comment. It plays very well with Gatsby, and was really easy to setup since it can be freely added to a static website (such as this one). You can find it here: https://disqus.com/

Why take it down?

Recently though, I reflected on the fact that, in today's society, we tend all too much to the unique model of social medias and that this website is not such a plateform. At the beginning, I wanted to allow my visitors to comment on my pages, not so much because I felt it was the right thing to do, but rather because everyone does it.

It is not that I don't want to share or discuss with people about my ideas and opinions. By all means, if you want to talk to me about anything that I have said, contact me by email and I will gladly answer or discuss with you.

My feeling is revolving around the idea that this website are more about sharing my opinions and views not for the sake of being seen but as a testimony. If some people start to think or talk about it, it would be great indeed, because it means that my thoughts were worth being shared or talked about. However those thoughts are not published for the sake of being "socialized". They are not shared for the sake of being seen. They are shared for the sake of making think.

End note

This is not a very fancy article. It will however serve as a proof for my own usage of what the website was before and what it became of it. I may or may not develop some of the thoughts here... Stay tuned!
